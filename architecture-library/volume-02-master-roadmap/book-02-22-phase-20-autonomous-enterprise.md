# Book 02-22 - Phase 20 Autonomous Enterprise

**Version:** v2 draft
**Status:** Draft
**Document ID:** MAL-V02-B022


## Purpose
Define the long-horizon target where Mariam can coordinate substantial enterprise work with bounded autonomy.

## Scope
Covers autonomous planning, execution supervision, enterprise simulation, self-improvement loops, risk controls, and executive oversight.

## Strategic Objective
Reach high-leverage autonomy only after governance, knowledge, runtime, and evaluation foundations are mature.

## Systems Included
- Autonomous planning
- Supervised execution
- Enterprise simulation
- Risk scoring
- Executive oversight
- Self-improvement controls

## Main Deliverables
- Autonomous enterprise concept blueprint
- Autonomy maturity model
- Risk and escalation framework
- Simulation requirements
- Executive control surfaces

## Dependencies
- All earlier roadmap phases.
- Governance, security, AI, swarm, and evolution principles.
- Mature evaluation and runtime systems.

## Risks
- Premature autonomy can create unacceptable business and security risk.
- Complex decisions may become impossible to explain.
- Self-improvement loops can amplify errors without controls.

## Acceptance Criteria
- Autonomous behavior remains bounded, observable, and reversible.
- High-impact decisions require human-defined authority.
- Simulation and evaluation precede production autonomy.

## Implementation Notes
- Treat this as a long-term maturity phase, not an early product promise.
- Use autonomy levels and explicit permission boundaries.
- Require evidence before expanding autonomous scope.
